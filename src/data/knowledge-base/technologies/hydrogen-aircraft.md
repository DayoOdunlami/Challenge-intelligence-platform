# Hydrogen-Powered Aircraft Technology

**Source:** [Launching Hydrogen-Powered Aviation Report](https://hydrogeninaviation.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Launching-Hydrogen-Powered-Aviation-Report.pdf)  
**Date:** March 2024  
**Organization:** Hydrogen in Aviation (HIA)  
**Last Updated:** 2024-03

## Overview

Hydrogen-powered flight offers significant potential to deliver Net Zero aviation. When produced using carbon-free energy, hydrogen flights will have no carbon emissions. Hydrogen also has the potential to deliver a cost-effective replacement for short haul aviation.

## Technology Status

### Current State

> "The use of hydrogen in aviation is already underway. There are already two manufacturers in the UK of hydrogen-powered aircraft, Airbus and ZeroAvia, and some of these aircraft are flying today."

### Key Manufacturers

1. **Airbus**
   - Committed to developing commercial scale hydrogen-powered aircraft
   - Target: Entering service in 2035
   - ZEROe aircraft programme

2. **ZeroAvia**
   - UK-based hydrogen-electric aircraft developer
   - Aircraft already flying today
   - Focus on regional and narrowbody aircraft

## Technical Characteristics

### Energy Density

> "While its energy-per-unit mass is three times higher than traditional kerosene-based jet fuel, its volumetric density is much lower."

**Key Quote from Guillaume Faury, CEO of Airbus:**
> "This leads to significant design challenges, but those whose solution is at reach with current technological capabilities."

### Propulsion Options

Hydrogen enables multiple options for aircraft propulsion:

1. **Direct Combustion**
   - Hydrogen burned in modified gas turbine engines
   - Similar to current jet engines but using hydrogen fuel

2. **Fuel-Cell Electricity**
   - Hydrogen converted to electricity via fuel cells
   - Powers electric motors for propulsion

3. **Combination (Hybrid)**
   - Combination of both direct combustion and fuel-cell electricity
   - Optimizes efficiency and power output

4. **Synthetic Fuels Feedstock**
   - Hydrogen as feedstock for sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) production
   - Enables low-carbon synthetic kerosene

- Mainstream adoption of hydrogen-powered aircraft

## Development Challenges

### Design Challenges

1. **Volumetric Density**
   - Lower volumetric density than kerosene
   - Requires larger fuel tanks or different aircraft design
   - Solutions within current technological capabilities

2. **Storage**
   - Liquid hydrogen requires cryogenic storage
   - Infrastructure for liquid hydrogen at airports
   - Safety considerations

3. **Distribution**
   - Hydrogen production and distribution infrastructure
   - Pipeline or transport to airports
   - Refueling systems

### Infrastructure Requirements

> "For such an aircraft programme to be successful the maturity of its surrounding hydrogen ecosystem will be at least as important as the aircraft development."

Key infrastructure needs:
- Hydrogen production facilities
- Distribution infrastructure
- Airport refueling systems
- Regulatory environment
