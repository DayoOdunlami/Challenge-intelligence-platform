# Hydrogen in Aviation (HIA)

**Source:** [Launching Hydrogen-Powered Aviation Report](https://hydrogeninaviation.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Launching-Hydrogen-Powered-Aviation-Report.pdf)  
**Date:** March 2024  
**Established:** Summer 2023  
**Last Updated:** 2024-03

## Overview

Hydrogen in Aviation (HIA) is an industry-led coalition established in summer 2023, bringing together leading organisations operating across the aviation value chain. The coalition represents airlines, airports, manufacturers, infrastructure providers, and energy producers.

## Leadership

- **Chair:** Johan Lundgren, CEO of easyJet
- **Members:** Airbus, Bristol Airport, easyJet, GKN Aerospace, Ørsted, Rolls-Royce, ZeroAvia

## Mission

> "Our core mission is to ensure the UK maximises its opportunity to be a global leader in hydrogen-powered aviation."

## Core Belief

> "Hydrogen-powered flight has the potential to deliver Net Zero aviation in a way that preserves the huge social and economic benefits of flying and continues to further enrich people's lives."

## Working Groups

HIA established three working groups to develop recommendations:

1. **Investment Group**
   - Focused on funding mechanisms and R&D investment
   - Identified milestones for investment in hydrogen aviation technology

2. **Infrastructure Group**
   - Focused on hydrogen production, distribution, and airport infrastructure
   - Developed recommendations for infrastructure development

3. **Policy and Regulation Group**
   - Focused on regulatory frameworks and government policy
   - Engaged with stakeholders including CAA, DESNZ, DBT, DfT, HMT

## Key Activities

- Research to identify milestones
- Engagement with relevant stakeholders
- Production of recommendations
- Advocacy for hydrogen-powered aviation

## Focus Areas

1. **Technology Development**
   - Supporting R&D in hydrogen-powered aircraft
   - Test infrastructure and capabilities

2. **Infrastructure**
   - Hydrogen production and distribution
   - Airport infrastructure for hydrogen fueling

3. **Policy & Regulation**
   - Regulatory framework development
   - Government policy coordination

4. **Skills Development**
   - Workforce planning
   - Skills strategy development

## Member Organizations

### Airbus
- **Role:** Aircraft manufacturer
- **Commitment:** First ZEROe aircraft by 2035, powered by hydrogen
- **SAF Target:** Certify all civil aircraft to run on 100% SAF by 2030

### Bristol Airport
- **Role:** Airport operator
- **Commitment:** Leading player in facilitating zero-carbon flight
- **Target:** Enable commercial hydrogen flights by 2035

### easyJet
- **Role:** Airline
- **Commitment:** Support advancement of hydrogen powered flight
- **Target:** Net Zero by 2050 (hydrogen plays key role)

### GKN Aerospace
- **Role:** Aerospace manufacturer
- **Commitment:** Majority of R&D expenditure in decarbonisation technologies
- **Focus:** Hydrogen propulsion technologies

### Ørsted
- **Role:** Energy producer
- **Commitment:** Create world that runs entirely on green energy
- **Focus:** Green hydrogen production

### Rolls-Royce
- **Role:** Engine manufacturer
- **Commitment:** Net Zero across value chain
- **Approach:** Efficient gas turbines, sustainable fuels, new technologies

### ZeroAvia
- **Role:** Hydrogen-electric aircraft developer
- **Commitment:** Deliver clean future of flight
- **Target:** Regional zero-emissions aircraft this decade, narrowbody in 2030s
